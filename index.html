Autonomous-Driving-Platform

The goal of this Startup is to build a self-driving vehicle (prototype), using computer vision and deep neural networks, to navigate in various environments.

Following milestones are important in achieving this goal.

  • Part 1: Car Hacking
  • Part 2: Data Collection
  • Part 3: AI Training
  • Part 4: Integration and Testing

Methdology

Car Hacking

The electric car is basically a mechatronic system. The steering and throttle are controlled by the driver. To autonomously drive the car, a communication system needs to be established in between the car and an on-board computer.

Data Collection

To train the artificial neural network, a large amount of data is required to be collected. At this stage, additional hardware (camera and LiDAR), will be connected to the car's on-board computer which will act as an input to its decision-making.

AI Training

Different CNN architectures such as PilotNet, ResNet, or Inception will be investigated and modified for this application. These networks will be trained via collected data to predict the steering angle and the throttle from the inputs received through the camera and LiDAR.

Integration and Testing

The performance of each network will be compared by testing them under different environments such as sidewalks, driveways, and parking lots. All the trained networks will undergo the same testing conditions and the final network will be selected based on its performance.
